================================================================
DATA DICTIONARY
Whale-Vessel Disturbance Risk — California Coast
================================================================

----------------------------------------------------------------
1. NOAA WHALEWATCH
----------------------------------------------------------------
Source:   NOAA Fisheries WhaleWatch (whalewatch.noaa.gov)
Format:   CSV, one file per month
Files:    WhaleWatchPredictions_YYYY_MM.csv
Coverage: 2020–2025 (some months missing — see whale_watch_missing_months.txt)
Grid:     0.25-degree resolution, US West Coast

Each row represents one grid cell for one month.

COLUMNS
-------
longitude   Longitude of grid cell center. Raw values are in 0–360 degree
            convention (e.g. 225 = -135 in standard -180/180 convention).
            Converted to standard convention in processing by subtracting
            360 from values above 180.

latitude    Latitude of grid cell center, in standard -90/90 convention.
            Ranges from approximately 30 to 49 degrees North.

bathy       Water depth at the grid cell center, in meters. Negative values
            indicate depth below sea surface. Sourced from bathymetric
            datasets used in the WhaleWatch model.

bathyrms    Bathymetric roughness — standard deviation of depth within the
            grid cell. Higher values indicate more varied seafloor terrain.

sst         Sea surface temperature in degrees Celsius. Derived from
            satellite remote sensing products. A key predictor of whale
            distribution as whales follow productive, cooler upwelling zones.

chl         Chlorophyll-a concentration in mg/m³. A proxy for phytoplankton
            abundance and ocean productivity. High chlorophyll areas
            indicate productive feeding habitat that attracts whales.

ssh         Sea surface height in meters, derived from satellite altimetry.
            Relates to ocean circulation patterns and mesoscale features
            such as eddies that concentrate prey.

sshrms      Sea surface height variability — standard deviation of SSH
            within the grid cell. Indicates dynamic oceanographic features.

month       Month of the observation (1–12).

year        Year of the observation.

fitmean     Modeled whale density estimate — the predicted number of whales
            per 100 km². This is the primary model output. NA values indicate
            the model could not produce an estimate for that cell-month due
            to insufficient satellite input data. NA rows are excluded from
            analysis and should NOT be treated as zero.

sdfit       Standard deviation of the whale density estimate (fitmean).
            Represents model uncertainty. Higher values indicate less
            confident predictions.

percent     Modeled whale presence probability, expressed as a percentage
            (0–100). Represents the probability that at least one whale is
            present in the grid cell in a given month. This is the primary
            input to the disturbance risk calculation. NA follows same
            rules as fitmean.

density     Alternative density metric. May differ from fitmean depending
            on model version. Check units before use.

sddens      Standard deviation of the density estimate.

upper       Upper confidence bound of the density estimate.

NOTE ON NA VALUES
-----------------
NA values in fitmean, percent, and related model output columns mean the
WhaleWatch model did not run for that cell in that month. This is typically
caused by gaps in the satellite oceanographic input data (SST, chlorophyll,
SSH). NA does not mean whales were absent — it means no prediction was
possible. These rows are dropped before analysis.


----------------------------------------------------------------
2. GLOBAL FISHING WATCH — AIS VESSEL PRESENCE
----------------------------------------------------------------
Source:   Global Fishing Watch 4Wings API, public-global-presence:latest
          (globalfishingwatch.org)
Format:   CSV, one file per month
Files:    gfw_YYYY_MM.csv
Coverage: 2020–2025
Bounding: lon -135 to -115, lat 30 to 49 (US West Coast)
Grid:     0.1-degree resolution (LOW spatial resolution setting)

Each row represents one grid cell, for one vessel type, one speed band,
one flag state, for one month. Multiple rows can share the same lat/lon
if different flags or speed bands are present in that cell.

COLUMNS
-------
lat         Latitude of grid cell center in standard -90/90 convention.
            Values are at 0.1-degree resolution (e.g. 37.8, 38.1).

lon         Longitude of grid cell center in standard -180/180 convention.
            Values are at 0.1-degree resolution (e.g. -122.5, -124.3).

hours       Vessel presence in hours. Calculated as one AIS position per
            vessel per hour, aggregated over all vessels of the given type
            and speed band in the cell for the month. This is the primary
            vessel exposure metric used in the risk calculation.

date        Month-year string for the observation (e.g. "2020-01").

flag        ISO3 country code of the vessel flag state (e.g. USA, PAN, LBR).
            Included because the API requires grouping by flag. For risk
            analysis, hours are summed across all flags per cell.

vesselIDs   Number of distinct vessels contributing to the hours total
            in this row.

vessel_type Vessel category, one of:
              cargo   — bulk carriers, container ships, general cargo
              fishing — commercial fishing vessels of all gear types
            (tanker was excluded due to very low presence in study area)

speed_band  Speed range of the vessel in knots, one of:
              <2      — nearly stationary, anchored or drifting
              2-4     — very slow
              4-6     — slow
              6-10    — moderate
              10-15   — normal transit speed, elevated strike risk
              15-25   — fast transit, high strike risk
              >25     — very fast, highest strike risk
            Speed bands are used to compute a weighted exposure score
            where faster vessels contribute more to collision risk.

year        Year of the observation (integer, added during processing).

month       Month of the observation (integer 1–12, added during processing).

NOTE ON SPEED WEIGHTING
-----------------------
Literature on whale-vessel collisions identifies approximately 10 knots
as a critical threshold above which strikes become more likely to be
lethal (Conn and Silber 2013, van der Hoop et al. 2015). Speed bands
are used to construct a weighted vessel exposure score in the analysis,
where higher speed bands receive greater weight in the risk index.

NOTE ON GRID ALIGNMENT
-----------------------
GFW data is on a 0.1-degree grid. WhaleWatch data is on a 0.25-degree
grid. Before joining, GFW cell hours are aggregated to the 0.25-degree
whale watch grid by snapping each GFW cell to its nearest whale watch
grid point.
